Design Concept • 2-Day Sprint

CREATIFY

Your space to create without limits. A minimalist dashboard designed to revolutionize how creatives organize ideas, replacing rigid text documents with a dynamic, multimedia workspace.

UI / UX Design Figma Ideation Sprint

The Challenge

Most creatives—copywriters, advertisers, and designers—are forced to draft their concepts in standard word processors. Because these interfaces are flat and built for formal documents, they offer zero inspiration and make it incredibly difficult to organize multimedia references by client or project.

The Goal: Design a "Dashboard" platform in a 2-day sprint that allows users to easily organize their ideas, backed by a clean, minimalist UI and disruptive, non-linear navigation.

KEY FEATURES

  • ✦ Multimedia Integration: Seamlessly mix text, images, and visual references in the same environment.
  • ✦ Smart Organization: A robust tagging system and intuitive sidebar to sort files by client and project instantly.
  • ✦ Collaboration Tools: Online cooperation, change tracking, and an integrated AI widget to assist ideation.

Core Experiences

I designed four primary interaction pillars that turn passive reading into active participation.

Micro-InteractionAdd Your Seed

A core interaction allowing users to leave a digital tribute ("Add your seed"). This was designed to feel organic, giving users a tangible, visual way to participate in the memorial and watch the community support grow in real-time.

Data VisualizationA Map of Memories

An interactive geographical visualization. By grounding the data in real locations, the map helps users comprehend the scale and reality of the events. Filtering tools allow users to search by region or year without feeling overwhelmed.

Content LayoutDiscover Their Stories

A dedicated, distraction-free reading environment designed to humanize the statistics. Focuses heavily on legibility and emotional resonance through careful typography, ample whitespace, and integrated "Voices of the past" audio controls.
