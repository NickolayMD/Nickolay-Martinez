Project Overview

6402+ is not just a platform; it's a digital sanctuary. Designed to honor victims and preserve history, the interface guides users through a sensitive, empathetic journey bridging the gap between grief, data visualization, and collective remembrance.

The Challenge

How to visually and respectfully represent over 6,402 victims without reducing them to mere statistics or overwhelming the user with heavy data. The platform needed to feel human, accessible, and emotionally resonant.

The Solution

A carefully structured web experience that uses symbolic interactions—like planting a "seed of memory"—alongside a geographical map and audio-visual archives to ground the data in real human stories.

My Role & Contributions

In the 6402+ project, my focus centered on bridging the gap between product strategy and technical execution. As the Product Manager, I guided the project's overarching direction, ensuring that our features consistently aligned with the highly sensitive nature of the memorial and the emotional needs of our users.

Unity Developer: The Map of Memories

Technically, my main role was developing the core interactive product—the "Map of Memories" (Un mapa de memorias). Using Unity, I was responsible for programming the map environment, plotting the data, and ensuring a seamless, respectful, and interactive geographic visualization of the victims' stories.

Design Process

Designing a platform dealing with memory and grief required a meticulous and highly sensitive approach. We followed a structured design thinking framework to ensure every interaction felt respectful.

01. Empathize

Understanding the emotional weight of the survivors and public.

02. Define

Establishing boundaries for respectful data visualization.

03. Ideate

Creating the "Seeds" concept and interaction models.

04. Test & Iterate

Ensuring accessibility and appropriate emotional pacing.

Core Experiences

I designed four primary interaction pillars that turn passive reading into active participation.

Micro-InteractionAdd Your Seed

A core interaction allowing users to leave a digital tribute ("Add your seed"). This was designed to feel organic, giving users a tangible, visual way to participate in the memorial and watch the community support grow in real-time.

Data VisualizationA Map of Memories

An interactive geographical visualization. By grounding the data in real locations, the map helps users comprehend the scale and reality of the events. Filtering tools allow users to search by region or year without feeling overwhelmed.

Content LayoutDiscover Their Stories

A dedicated, distraction-free reading environment designed to humanize the statistics. Focuses heavily on legibility and emotional resonance through careful typography, ample whitespace, and integrated "Voices of the past" audio controls.

Key Takeaways

Designing 6402+ taught me that UX is not just about reducing friction; sometimes, it's about creating meaningful friction.

By carefully pacing the user journey, we allowed space for reflection. The biggest challenge was balancing comprehensive data with emotional sensitivity, resulting in a platform that doesn't just display information, but facilitates a collective grieving and remembrance process.

The Final Result

The culmination of this research and design process is a fully interactive prototype that brings the 6402+ memorial to life. Experience the sensitive micro-interactions, explore the map, and discover the stories firsthand.
